Walt Disney

The Man Behind the Mouse

Walt and Mickey making guests smile at Disneyland

    A timeline of Walt's life:

  • 1901 - Born Walter Elias Disney on December 5th in Chicago, Illinois
  • 1918 - Attempted to join the United States Army, but was too young. Forged his age in order to serve as an ambulance driver for the Red Cross
  • 1921 - Established Laugh-O-Gram Studio in Kansas City, Missouri to produce cartoon shorts for local theaters
  • 1923 - Established the Disney Brothers Studio (later to become The Walt Disney Company) with his brother Roy in Hollywood, California
  • 1928 - Created Mickey Mouse with fellow animator Ub Iwerks - Originally chose the name "Mortimer," but Walt's wife Lillian preferred "Mickey"
  • 1937 - Snow White and the Seven Dwarfs is released to great acclaim, launching the studio's "Golden Age of Animation"
  • 1955 - After being in some form of development since 1948, Disneyland - considered by many to be the world's first theme park - opens in Anaheim, California
  • 1965 - Announces plans for Walt Disney World in Florida - this resort destination would not open until 1971
  • 1966 - Dies of complications due to lung cancer

"All our dreams can come true, if we have the courage to pursue them."
